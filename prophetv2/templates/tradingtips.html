Candle Stick
  • A green candlestick shows that the opening price on that day was lower than the closing price that day (i.e. the price moved up during the day).
  • A red candlestick shows that the opening price was higher than the closing price that day (i.e. the price moved down during the day).
Trendlines
A trendline is a straight line that connects two or more price points and may be extended into the future to act as a line of support or resistance.
  • A trendline drawn that has a positive slope can be called an upward trend. (1)
  • A trendline drawn that has a negative slope can be called a downward trend. (2)
  • A trendline that connects price lows is called a support line.
  • A trendline that connects price highs is called a resistance line

Stock prices
How stock prices are determined
  • A company's initial stock price is determined by a 3rd party (typically an investment bank) based on the company’s market cap when it first IPO (Initial Public Offering).
  • Once a company goes public and its shares start trading on a stock exchange, its share price is determined by supply and demand in the market. If there is a high demand for its shares, the price will increase.
  • On the other hand, shareholders might sell the stock if they feel that the company’s potential looks dubious which lowers down the price of a stock.

Dollar Cost Averaging
DCA is a strategy in which instead of making one lump-sum purchase of a financial instrument, the investment is divided into smaller sums that are invested separately at regular predetermined intervals until the full amount of capital is exhausted.
  • Dollar Cost Averaging is investing the same amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the price of a security.
  • This strategy, with its potential to mitigate timing risk, may also help investors or traders to lower their average cost per share and reduce the impact of volatility on their portfolios.
Stock chart patterns
Chart pattern analysis is a part of technical analysis that can help traders or analysts to predict the future direction of the markets and spot potential trend reversals based on historical price data.
  • It is used as a set of predefined patterns that are formed by moving market prices. The most common stock chart patterns include double tops, double bottoms, and head and shoulders.
  • Double Top
  • A stock chart pattern in which the price will experience a peak, before retracing back to a level of support. It will then rise up once more, almost identical to the previous peak, before reversing back more permanently against the prevailing trend.
  • Double Bottom
  • A stock chart pattern in which the price will drop below a level of support. It will then rise to a level of resistance, before dropping again. Finally, the trend will reverse and start to shift towards an uptrend as the market becomes more bullish. Basically, this chart pattern is the opposite of the double top chart pattern.
  • Head and shoulders
  • A stock chart pattern in which a large peak has a slightly smaller peak on either side of it, normally, the first and third peak will be smaller than the second.
  • However, they will all fall back to the same level of support, otherwise known as the ‘neckline’. It is likely that when the third peak has fallen to the support level, it will break out into a bearish downtrend.

Bullish vs Bearish
In the investing world, the terms "bull" and "bear" are frequently used to refer to market conditions
  • Bullish investors acts with a belief that prices will rise
  • Bearish investors act with the belief prices will fall.
  • Patterns and trends in major stock market indexes are often described in bullish vs. bearish terms. And as an investor, the direction of the market is a major force that has a huge impact on your portfolio
  • In a bull market, there is strong demand and weak supply for securities. This leads to more investors wising to buy as compared to selling the stock. Therefore this will result in the stock price going up due to the demand. In the bear market the opposite happens. As more are looking to sell the buy. Which results in the price of the stock to drop.
  • In a bull market, an investor would normally take advantage of the rising price and buy stocks as early as possible so that he/she can sell it off once it had reached a peak according to the investor. During the bull market, any losses should be minor and temporary; an investor can typically actively and confidently invest in more equity with a higher probability of making a return. In a bear market, however, the chance of losses is greater because prices are continually losing value and the end is often not in sight. Therefore it's advised to not invest in such a situation and wait for a sign of a bullish market.
  • In a Bear market there is a weak demand for a stock and plentiful supply which lowers stock value. This causes a snowball effect of selling, affecting the stock value further.
  • When the market is bearish, it is more likely to make a loss due to a dip in prices and the end is often not in sight. Therefore it's advised to be more prudent with your investments and wait for a bullish market.
Basic Shorting/Long
What does it mean to long or to short?
  • Shorting a stock means opening a position by borrowing shares that you do not own and then selling them to another investor.
  • It is a bearish stock position, meaning you might want to short a stock if you feel strongly that its share price is going to fall down. In order to sell short, an investor has to borrow the stock through their brokerage company from someone who owns it. The investor sells the stock subsequently, retaining the profit made.
  • Having a long position in a security means that you own the security.
  • Investors maintain “long” security positions in the expectation that the stock will rise in value in the future
Max Buying Power
What does it mean to long or to short?
  • Max Buying Power is the money an investor has available to buy securities in a trading context. It is calculated by the total cash held in the brokerage account plus all available margin.
Liquidity
Liquidity is the measure of how easily an asset can be exchanged for monetary value.
Investments can be said to have high liquidity if it can be converted to cash quickly as they are generally easy to access and trade.
  • Liquid market - It's when there are alway investors readily available in the market welling to trade securities at every price level resulting in a market with high trading activities
  • Liquid asset - It's an asset that can be easily turned into cash
  • Liquidity is extremely important when considering your trading positions and the ability to exit the market. Therefore liquidity can be said to ensure that you can easily get in, or out of the market.
